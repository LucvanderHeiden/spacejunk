NO MORE SPACE TRAVEL?

Humanity has been using satellites for more than half a decade for, among other applications, communication, observation, navigation and science. These satellites are sent up into space and are then placed in an orbit around the earth. This means they must gain enough speed to outrun the earth's gravity so that they are able to orbit the earth almost indefinitely. However, the amount of satellites currently in orbit around the earth is getting out of hand and if we don't collectively do something about this, the consequences will be very severe.

01

IMPACT ON DAILY LIFE

Not being able to travel into space anymore would be a huge loss for us all, but why is this even happening? Currently earth is being orbited by millions of objects. Some of these are space stations and satellites currently in use for a number of reasons. However, a large part of the space is being taken up by literal junk... spacejunk. This consists of old sattelites, broken equipment or simply debris from either of these. For objects to stay in orbit around the earth they have to travel at enormous speeds. These objects travel at around 7 kilometers per second. At that speed it would take you about one and a half hours to travel a complete circle around the earth.
